Reaffirms all relevant resolutions relating to the mandate of the Peacebuilding Commission, and, mindful of the important role of the Commission, also reaffirms the need to strengthen its fulfilment by: (a) Requesting the Peacebuilding Commission to establish a detailed, predictable and flexible annual programme of work reflecting, inter alia, its country- specific, regional and thematic issues, while also taking into consideration the programmes of work of the General Assembly, the Security Council and the Economic and Social Council, as appropriate; (b) Requesting the Peacebuilding Commission, in line with its mandate, to serve as a platform to support Member States, upon their request, to strengthen their national peacebuilding and sustaining peace efforts by: (i) Assisting Member States in building their national capacities to promote, develop, where they do not exist, and implement their voluntary and nationally owned prevention and peacebuilding strategies, efforts and other related frameworks in accordance with their national priorities and needs, including by sharing good practices and lessons learned; (ii) Inviting international financial institutions and regional development banks to engage in relevant country-specific discussions, upon request by the country concerned, including on nationally led and owned prevention and peacebuilding strategies, priorities and other related frameworks and, where appropriate and in line with their governance arrangements, to consider providing additional resources in support of their implementation; (c) Taking note of the decision of the Security Council to consider, where appropriate and on a case-by-case basis, referring countries transitioning off its agenda, at their request and with their consent, to the Peacebuilding Commission, with due respect for national ownership and mindful of the benefits of sustained engagement with the Commission; (d) Requesting the Peacebuilding Commission to strengthen its impact through regular follow-up, monitoring and assessment of the implementation of its outcomes, and diversified modalities of engagement with relevant stakeholders; (e) Requesting the Peacebuilding Commission to continue serving as a platform to convene all relevant actors, and gather analysis and expertise, including by leveraging information related to peacebuilding impact, with a view to advocating for developing and sharing good practices in peacebuilding and promoting system- wide coordination; Requests the Secretary-General, within available resources: (a) To continue the practice of sending an annual note verbale to Member States requesting the submission of their report on military expenditures; (b) To circulate annually a note verbale to Member States detailing which reports on military expenditures were submitted and are available online; (c) To continue consultations with relevant international bodies, with a view to ascertaining requirements for adjusting the present instrument in order to encourage wider participation, and to make recommendations, based on the outcome of those consultations and taking into account the views of Member States, on necessary changes to the content and structure of the standardized reporting system; (d) To encourage relevant international bodies and organizations to promote transparency of military expenditures and to consult with those bodies and organizations with emphasis on examining possibilities for enhancing complementarities among international and regional reporting systems and for exchanging related information between those bodies and the United Nations;
